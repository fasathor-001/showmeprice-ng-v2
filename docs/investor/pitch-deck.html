The Problem

Nigerians already buy online. They just don't trust the transaction.

Buyer fears

  • Fake listings, fake sellers
  • "DM for price" — no price upfront
  • Advance-payment scams
  • Disappearing vendors after payment
  • No recourse when things go wrong

Seller frustrations

  • Unserious "last price" buyers
  • Spam and fake payment claims
  • Time wasted on browsing, not buying
  • No way to signal serious buyer intent
  • Conversations off-platform = no record

Solution

Trust is built into the transaction flow.

Real prices

Every listing shows a price. No "DM for price" culture. Search becomes meaningful — price is the verb.

Phone-verified buyers

Nigerian-familiar phone-first signup via Termii. SMS verification + structured profile = real users, not bots.

Verified sellers

Manual identity verification at launch. NIN integration via Korapay Identity for scale. Verified badge as trust signal.

In-app messaging

Free buyers chat inside ShowMePrice. Creates a reviewable trail for moderation and dispute resolution.

Pro fast lane

Pro buyers unlock WhatsApp + phone contact, get SMS alerts, show as "Pro buyer inquiry" in seller inboxes.

Escrow protection

Eligible high-value transactions (₦50K+) flow through escrow. Seller paid after buyer confirms. 1.5% + ₦100 fee.

Product Flow

Two sides, one trust-enforced conversation.

Buyer journey

  1. Browse listings (real prices visible)
  2. Save listings · bookmarks
  3. Phone-first signup (Termii OTP)
  4. Message seller in-app
  5. Upgrade to Pro · reveal contact
  6. Use escrow on ₦50K+ purchases
  7. Report / block if needed

Seller journey

  1. Create business profile · verify
  2. List products with required price
  3. Receive verified inquiries · inbox grouped by listing
  4. Use quick replies · Nigerian-flavored templates
  5. See Pro buyer badge → reply faster
  6. Accept escrow on high-value sale
  7. Get paid post-confirmation

Revenue: ₦5,000/mo

Gross margin: 90%+
LTV at 6-mo retention: ₦30K · CAC target < ₦5K

Escrow (avg ₦180K txn)

Fee: 1.5% × ₦180K = ₦2,700 + ₦100 flat
Per-txn: ₦2,800
Direct cost: Paystack ~₦300
Gross margin: ~89%

Seller Premium (Phase F)

Revenue: ₦7,500/mo
Direct cost: Mostly software-fixed (no per-seller variable)
Gross margin: 92%+
Variable: support hours during onboarding

Marketplace economics improve with scale: SMS costs are throttled per-Pro-buyer, Paystack fees stay flat per transaction, and trust operations are amortized across rising transaction volume.

Go-To-Market

Lagos-first. Verified sellers before buyers.

Months 1–3

50–100 verified Lagos sellers in phones, laptops, electronics, appliances. Free Premium trial + onboarding help + verified badge. Founder-led outreach.

Months 4–6

Trust-led buyer acquisition. Content: "How to buy a used iPhone safely in Lagos." SEO + paid social on high-intent categories. First 5–15K active buyers.

Months 7–12

Pro buyer conversion + escrow adoption. Trial-to-paid funnel for Pro. First 100 escrow transactions. Reply-rate scorecards for sellers.

Months 13–18

City expansion. Abuja, Port Harcourt. Seller Premium launch (Phase F). Promoted listings as trust-weighted, not pay-to-rank.

Months 19–24

Logistics integration. Delivery partner pilots. Escrow + delivery bundles. Institution / bulk plan beta.

Defensibility

The Problem

Nigerian online commerce is already large — $9.35B in 2025 — but trust is the bottleneck. Buyers fear fake sellers and advance-payment scams; sellers waste time on unserious "DM for price" buyers. Most transactions migrate to WhatsApp the moment serious negotiation starts, taking the dispute trail with them.

The Solution

ShowMePrice builds trust into the transaction flow, not as a badge. Real prices required. Phone-verified buyers. In-app messaging by default (creates dispute trail). Pro buyers unlock direct WhatsApp/phone — a paid "serious buyer" signal sellers can see. Premium buyers access escrow on eligible high-value products (₦50,000+, 1.5% + ₦100 fee). Admin moderation with reports and blocks. Phase E ships the foundation; later phases add seller tools and logistics-backed fulfillment.

Market

Nigerian e-commerce: $9.35B (2025) → $18.68B (2031), ~12% CAGR. Social commerce: $2.04B → $3.96B by 2030. Mobile-first, 80%+ of online orders.

Wedge

Lagos-first. Phones, laptops, electronics, appliances, generators — categories where price transparency, seller credibility, and payment safety matter most. 50–100 verified sellers before aggressive buyer acquisition. We compete on trust density, not listing volume.

Business Model

  • Pro Buyer subscription — ₦5,000/mo or ₦45,000/yr — contact reveal, SMS alerts, Pro badge
  • Seller Premium (Phase F) — listing limits, analytics, badge priority
  • Escrow fee — 1.5% + ₦100 on eligible protected transactions
  • Promoted listings + institution plans + logistics (Phase G+)
